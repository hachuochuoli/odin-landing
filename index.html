In the golden sands of the Arabian Peninsula, centuries ago, a wandering Sufi mystic from Yemen discovered the energizing effect of coffee beans. As the story goes, the mystic observed spirited birds feasting on the bright red berries of a wild bush and decided to try them himself. The invigorating effect was immediate. He shared this discovery with a monk who, finding it kept him awake during long nights of prayer, began to spread the word. Soon, coffee became a staple in Sufi monasteries across the Middle East, used to sustain worshippers during their devotions. This mystical origin rooted coffee deeply in Islamic culture, making it more than just a drink, but a brew of spiritual awakening and cultural heritage."
